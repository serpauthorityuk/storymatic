The Art of Winemaking at Tertre Roteboeuf

At Château Le Tertre Roteboeuf, making wine is like creating a masterpiece. From when the grapes are picked to when the wine is finally bottled, every step is done with great care to ensure the wines are top-notch and full of character.

The journey starts with turning grape sugar into alcohol in concrete tanks. This setup helps keep temperatures just right and gently pulls out all the good flavours and tannins from the grapes.

Then, for 22 to 24 months, these wines get better with age in new oak barrels. During this time, they gain more layers of taste, become structurally sounder, and blend their flavours beautifully together. The fresh oak gives them an extra touch of richness along with hints of vanilla and spice.

From Harvest to Fermentation

At Château Le Tertre Roteboeuf, the path from picking grapes to fermenting them is super important for making their wine. They make sure to pick the grapes when they're just right, full of flavour.

After being picked, each grape goes through a process where it's sorted out and then taken off its stem before getting lightly squashed. Then, these crushed grapes are moved into special tanks to start turning sugar into alcohol.

Over at this chateau, they use concrete tanks for fermentation because it helps keep temperatures steady and makes sure flavours and tannins - those things that give wine its taste and feel - come out just right without losing any freshness or complexity.

Paying close attention every step of the way from harvest to fermentation really pays off in creating top-notch wines with lots of layers of taste at Chateau Le Tertre Roteboeuf.
